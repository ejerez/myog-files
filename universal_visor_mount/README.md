# Universal PST550 Visor Adapter

This is a free-to-use model that can be used to mount the excellent Decathlon PST550/950
visors to any helmet.

## Features:

* Great, unimpeded field of view, awesome color choices and different S ratings from Decathlon.
* Relatively cheap.
* Locks in two positions, "deployed" and "retracted".

## What else do you need:

* A helmet (duh!) that you'd like to add a visor to.
* A PST550/950 visor from Decathlon (they're the
same).
* A "Repair Kit PST550/950". This consists of the pieces that slide on the universal adapter, screws to attach them,
o-ring style rubber bands, some small circular pieces, and the top covers.
* A decent 3D printer that can print at least PETG, and some PETG ready to be used!

## How to print:

Make sure you print it in mm (stl file is in mm) and using PETG or some other sturdy and heat resistant
filament like ABS. PLA is very likely to melt or suffer from brittle failure if you attempt to use it
for such a thin piece that experiences such forces.

## How to mount:

* You'll have to first assemble everything: fit the circular piece from the repair kit on the screw hole of the adapter,
make sure the shapes match and lock into each other, then screw the screw on, and add the rubber band (it should hold the sliding
piece and the screw head together, alowing the entire mechanism to lock into two different positions (see assembly picture!). Then add the top covers and
snap the whole mechanism into the visor.
* Align the visor carefully: Attach it temporarily with something sticky but removable (like a gentle double-sided tape), or just hold it carefully. It should be
able to snap into both the retracted and deployed position without getting overly deformed (see pictures!). Then mark the correct locations and
remove the temporary attachment if you used it. This is the most finnicky part of the process, don't expect the adapter pieces to sit flush against the helmet surface,
they most likely won't!
* Now that you know the correct placement, carefully place the whole thing in the correct position (preferably snapped in "retracted" mode, folded up),
and start gluing it. I used hot glue, you can use whatever you have available as long as it's able to build up some volume, since you want to fill in all
the space and make sure there are no gaps (and that the edges are smooth so it doesn't catch any lines!). Some small pieces of strong double sided tape can 
help a great deal in getting the whole thing to stick while the adapter pieces are misaligned with the surface of the helmet (stack as many as you need
until they stick the gapped side of the piece to the helmet, then fill in everything with glue)

## BAM, VISOR!
